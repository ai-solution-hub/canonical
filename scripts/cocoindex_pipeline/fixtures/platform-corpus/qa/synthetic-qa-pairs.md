# Bidder Clarification Questions and Answers — Office Supplies Framework (Synthetic)

The following question-and-answer pairs are synthetic clarifications published during a UK
public-sector office-supplies procurement. They contain no real company or client
information and exist as reference content for pipeline seam testing. The content routes
through the standard content branch and is expected to yield question-and-answer
extractions in addition to ordinary content chunks.

Q: What is the anticipated contract value and duration for this framework? A: The
framework has an estimated total value of nine hundred thousand pounds across an initial
term of three years, with an option to extend by a further twelve months at the buyer's
discretion. Call-off contracts are awarded under the framework terms.

Q: Are bidders required to hold a specific quality-management certification? A: Bidders
must hold, or demonstrate a credible plan to achieve within ninety days of award,
certification to ISO 9001 or an equivalent quality-management standard. Evidence is
requested at the selection stage and verified before contract signature.

Q: How will social value be evaluated within the award criteria? A: Social value carries a
ten per cent weighting in the award evaluation. Responses are scored against the buyer's
published social-value model, with particular emphasis on local employment, carbon
reduction, and support for small and medium-sized enterprises in the supply chain.

Q: Is electronic invoicing mandatory under the resulting contract? A: Yes. The successful
supplier must submit electronic invoices compatible with the buyer's finance platform
within thirty days of contract commencement. Paper invoices are not accepted and will be
returned unprocessed.

Q: What delivery service levels apply to standard consumable orders? A: Standard
consumable orders placed before the daily cut-off time are delivered the next working day
to the nominated delivery points. Performance against this service level is reported
monthly and is subject to the contractual key performance indicators.
