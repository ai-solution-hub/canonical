# Compliance, Governance and Certifications — Ridgeway Commercial Services Ltd (Synthetic)

## Overview

This compliance record is synthetic reference content for pipeline seam testing. It
describes three fictional certifications held by Ridgeway Commercial Services Ltd and
contains no real company or accreditation-body information beyond the generic ISO 9001
quality-management standard.

## ISO 9001:2015 — Quality Management

Ridgeway Commercial Services Ltd holds ISO 9001:2015 certification, covering its
quality-management system for order processing, warehousing, and delivery. The
certification is independently audited on an annual surveillance cycle, with a full
recertification audit every three years. Callum Rees, Head of Compliance and Quality, is
the certification's designated management representative and maintains the documented
procedures for order accuracy, returns, and complaint handling referenced in the company's
quality manual.

## Facilities Assurance Standard (FAS 2100)

The Facilities Assurance Standard (FAS 2100) is a sector accreditation covering
health-and-safety management, site-access control, and incident reporting for suppliers
delivering facilities services into regulated environments such as NHS estates. Ridgeway
achieved FAS 2100 accreditation in 2021 and renews it every two years through an
independent assessment body. The accreditation was a mandatory selection-stage requirement
in Ridgeway's engagement with St Aldhelm's NHS Foundation Trust.

## Sustainable Supply Chain Mark (SSCM)

The Sustainable Supply Chain Mark (SSCM) evidences a supplier's carbon-reporting practice,
packaging take-back scheme, and social-value delivery across its supply chain. Ridgeway
holds SSCM accreditation at the "advanced" tier, reflecting the GreenCycle Furniture
Programme's remanufacturing and take-back performance and the company's published
carbon-reduction trajectory. The SSCM accreditation is reassessed annually and was cited
by Corvedale Academies Trust as a decisive factor in its award decision.

## Governance of certifications

All three certifications are tracked on a single compliance register, with renewal dates
monitored by the Compliance and Quality department. A lapsed or downgraded certification
triggers an immediate notification to Naomi Whitfield, Managing Director, and to every
account manager holding a live contract that named the certification at selection stage.
