# Service Delivery Methodology — Managed Office Supplies (Synthetic)

## Overview

This methodology statement sets out how a supplier organises the delivery of a managed
office-supplies contract for a UK public-sector buyer. It is written as synthetic
reference content for pipeline seam testing and contains no real company or client
information. The approach follows established UK public-procurement practice under the
Procurement Act 2023 and the buyer's published social-value priorities.

## Mobilisation and transition

Mobilisation begins immediately after the contract award letter is issued. During the
first thirty days the supplier appoints a named account manager, confirms the delivery
catalogue, and agrees the ordering portal configuration with the buyer's category team. A
transition plan records every milestone, the responsible owner, and the agreed acceptance
criteria. Where an incumbent supplier is being replaced, open purchase orders are
reconciled and any outstanding deliveries are honoured to avoid a service gap.

## Order management and fulfilment

Orders are placed through a single web portal with punch-out support for the buyer's
finance system. Each requisition is checked against the contracted price list, the
framework catalogue, and the buyer's spend-approval thresholds before it is released to
the warehouse. Next-day delivery is the default service level for core stationery and
consumables, with a forty-eight-hour service level for bulk furniture items. Delivery
performance is measured against agreed key performance indicators and reported monthly.

## Sustainability and social value

The supplier prioritises recycled and remanufactured products wherever a compliant
equivalent exists, and consolidates deliveries to reduce vehicle movements and embodied
carbon. Packaging is taken back for reuse on a scheduled collection. Social-value
commitments include local apprenticeship places, supported-employment partnerships, and
quarterly reporting against the buyer's social-value framework. These commitments are
tracked as contractual deliverables rather than aspirations.

## Continuous improvement and governance

A quarterly service review brings together the account manager, the buyer's category lead,
and an operational delegate. The review examines performance against the key performance
indicators, open service issues, catalogue changes, and savings opportunities identified
through spend analysis. Lessons learned are fed back into the delivery model so that the
service improves measurably across the life of the contract.
