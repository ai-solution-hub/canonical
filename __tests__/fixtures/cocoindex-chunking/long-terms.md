# Contract Terms and Conditions

## Service Levels and Performance

The supplier shall provide the services described in the specification to the standards
set out in the service level agreement. The supplier shall provide quarterly performance
reports detailing the service levels achieved against the agreed key performance
indicators, and shall notify the contracting authority promptly of any anticipated
shortfall together with a remediation plan. Where the supplier persistently fails to meet
the agreed service levels, the contracting authority may apply service credits in
accordance with the agreed mechanism, and may, after following the escalation procedure,
terminate the contract for material breach.

## Charges and Payment

The charges payable under this contract are set out in the pricing schedule and are fixed
for the initial term unless varied by agreement in writing. The supplier shall submit
invoices monthly in arrears, supported by a breakdown of the services delivered. The
contracting authority shall pay valid and undisputed invoices within thirty days of
receipt. Where an invoice is disputed, the contracting authority shall pay the undisputed
portion and the parties shall resolve the dispute through the escalation procedure.

## Insurance and Liability

The supplier shall maintain, throughout the term of the contract and for a period of six
years thereafter, professional indemnity insurance, public liability insurance, and
employer's liability insurance, each to the minimum levels of cover specified in the
schedule. The supplier shall provide evidence of such cover on request. Neither party
shall be liable for any indirect or consequential loss, and the total aggregate liability
of each party under the contract shall be limited to the amount specified in the schedule,
save that nothing in the contract shall limit liability for death or personal injury
caused by negligence, for fraud, or for any other liability that cannot be limited by law.

## Termination and Exit

The contracting authority may terminate this contract for convenience on three months'
written notice. Either party may terminate the contract immediately on written notice if
the other party commits a material breach that is incapable of remedy, or that is capable
of remedy but is not remedied within thirty days of a written notice requiring remedy. On
termination or expiry, the supplier shall provide reasonable exit assistance, transfer all
data held on behalf of the contracting authority in an agreed format, and return or
securely destroy any confidential information in accordance with the agreed exit plan.

## Subcontracting and Assignment

The supplier shall not subcontract or assign any of its obligations under this contract
without the prior written consent of the contracting authority, such consent not to be
unreasonably withheld. Where consent is given, the supplier shall remain fully responsible
for the acts and omissions of its subcontractors as if they were its own. The supplier
shall ensure that any subcontract contains terms equivalent to those imposed on the
supplier under this contract, in particular in respect of confidentiality, data
protection, and audit.

## Compliance and Audit

The supplier shall comply with all applicable laws, regulations, and statutory guidance in
the performance of the services, including those relating to modern slavery, anti-bribery,
equality, and health and safety. The contracting authority, or its appointed
representative, may audit the supplier's records and premises relevant to the contract on
reasonable notice, and the supplier shall cooperate fully with any such audit. The
supplier shall maintain accurate and complete records relating to the services for a
period of six years after the end of the contract.

## Intellectual Property

All intellectual property rights in any deliverables created specifically for the
contracting authority under this contract shall, on payment, vest in the contracting
authority. The supplier shall retain ownership of its pre-existing intellectual property
and shall grant the contracting authority a perpetual, royalty-free, non-exclusive licence
to use such pre-existing material to the extent necessary to enjoy the benefit of the
deliverables. The supplier warrants that the deliverables will not infringe the
intellectual property rights of any third party and shall indemnify the contracting
authority against any claim arising from such infringement.

## Variation and Dispute Resolution

No variation of this contract shall be effective unless it is recorded in writing and
signed by an authorised representative of each party. The parties shall attempt in good
faith to resolve any dispute arising out of or in connection with the contract through the
escalation procedure set out in the schedule. Where a dispute cannot be resolved through
escalation, the parties may refer the matter to mediation before commencing court
proceedings. This contract is governed by the law of England and Wales, and the parties
submit to the exclusive jurisdiction of the courts of England and Wales.
