# example-client Design -- Brand Voice and Tone Observations

**Company:** Example Client Ltd **Source:** https://www.client.example/ and
related public sources **Research date:** 2026-02-14

## 9. Brand Voice and Tone Observations

### Overall Tone

example-client's brand voice is **professional yet approachable**, with a strong
undercurrent of **purpose and social mission**. The copy consistently bridges
the gap between technical competence and emotional engagement.

### Key Characteristics

1. **Purpose-led language:** Frequent references to "making a real difference,"
   "saving lives," and "social impact." The safeguarding mission is woven into
   everything, not treated as an afterthought. This is not just a software
   company -- it is a company driven by the belief that its tools contribute to
   child safety.

2. **Warm and human:** Uses first-person plural ("we," "our") extensively. Team
   members are described as "thinkers, makers and explorers." The company
   resists being "faceless" and emphasises being "an extension of your team."
   Staff are called out by first name in blog posts.

3. **Confident without being aggressive:** Uses phrases like "market leading,"
   "world class," "pioneering," and "industry-leading" but balances them with
   collaborative language ("working with," "supporting," "enabling"). The
   confidence is earned-credential-based (ISO certifications, quantified
   results) rather than aspirational.

4. **Data-driven claims:** Results are presented with specific percentages and
   numbers (630% increase, 95% reduction, 14,000 additional admissions). The
   copy favours proof points over vague benefit statements.

5. **Sector fluency:** The language demonstrates deep familiarity with
   safeguarding terminology (Sections 11, 175/157, KCSIE, Prevent Duty,
   multi-agency, ADASS). This positions example-client as an insider, not a vendor looking
   in from outside.

6. **Educational content marketing:** The blog produces regular
   thought-leadership content about safeguarding practice, accessibility
   standards, and public sector digital transformation. Titles are practical and
   benefit-oriented ("Transform your safeguarding training with a Learning
   Management System").

7. **Inclusive and empathetic:** Values language emphasises being "mindful,"
   acknowledging "every voice," and having "empathy for their community's
   challenges."

### Copy Patterns

- Headlines tend to be outcome-focused rather than feature-focused
- Blog posts use a semi-formal, authoritative but accessible register
- Product descriptions lead with the sector problem, then present the solution
- Case studies emphasise quantified impact rather than implementation details
- The exclamation mark in "example-client!" appears occasionally in informal contexts but
  the brand generally presents as "example-client" without it

### Tagline / Positioning Lines Observed

- "Sector-Specific Digital Products | Safeguarding Systems" (homepage title)
- "Thinkers, makers and explorers"
- "Making a real difference"
